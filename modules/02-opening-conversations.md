# Module 02: Opening Conversations

## Why the Opening Is Everything

You have 60-90 seconds to make someone feel like this conversation is worth their time. The goal is NOT to pitch. It is to make them feel good about talking to you and to start learning about their world.

---

## Warm Opens - From Website Chat Widget

When someone initiates contact they have already raised their hand. Do not waste it.

### Standard Warm Open
Hey [Name]! Great to meet you. Tell me a bit about your business - what do you do?

Why it works: casual and friendly, invites them to talk about themselves, gives you information to personalise the rest of the conversation.

### If They Ask About Price Immediately
Do not answer with price. Redirect: Of course! To make sure I give you the right info - what does your business do? I want to make sure we are talking about the right package for you.

---

## Cold Opens - WhatsApp, Phone, and In-Person

### WhatsApp Cold Open (After Seeing Their Business Online)
Hey [Name], hope you do not mind me reaching out! I saw [Business Name] online and noticed you do not have a website yet or your current one is not showing up on Google. I help Brisbane businesses get found online fast - usually up and running in 24 hours for 199. Keen to chat?

Key elements: context for why you are reaching out, acknowledge it is cold, lead with the insight not the product, low-friction close.

### WhatsApp Cold Open (From Referral)
Hey [Name]! [Referrer Name] mentioned I should reach out - they said you have been looking at getting a proper website sorted. I am Mason from Sun State Digital. We just got [Referrer Business] set up and they are already getting calls from Google. Happy to show you how it works if you have got 5 mins?

### Phone Cold Open
Hey [Name], this is Mason from Sun State Digital. I will keep it quick - I was looking at your Google listing today and noticed a couple of things that would probably be costing you jobs. Is now an OK time for 2 minutes?

### In-Person Open
Hey! Are you the owner? Love what you have got going on here. Quick question - are you getting much enquiry from Google, or is it mostly word of mouth?

## The Mirror Technique

Repeat the last 3 words someone said as a question.

Examples:
- Prospect: Yeah, we have been struggling to get leads lately.
- Mason: Struggling to get leads?

- Prospect: My old website just is not bringing anything in.
- Mason: Not bringing anything in?

Why it works: makes them feel heard, encourages them to elaborate, builds subconscious trust.

Use a slightly rising tone. Pause after mirroring and let them fill the silence.

## Building Rapport in Under 2 Minutes

### Step 1: Acknowledge Their World (15 seconds)
Show you understand their industry. Generic openers die here. Specific openers win.

Plumbing is a tough game right now - everyone is online, Google is packed with ads, it is hard to stand out without a system.

### Step 2: Find Common Ground (20 seconds)
Reference something you know about their business or location.

We have worked with a few plumbers in that area - same story, they were getting found but the website was not converting. Once we fixed that, the phones went crazy.

### Step 3: Ask the Right Question (30 seconds)
Can I ask - where does most of your work come from right now? Like, how do people find you?

This question is non-threatening, gets them talking, reveals their current situation, and tells you exactly where the gap is.

---

## The 3 Questions to Ask in Every Opening

### Question 1: What do you do?
Gives you their industry, service type, and how they describe their own business. Mirror their language back.
Follow-up: How long have you been doing that?

### Question 2: Where are you getting clients from now?
This is the diagnostic question. Their answer tells you everything:

- Mostly referrals: Not using digital at all. Frame: Referrals are gold but you are missing everyone who does not know you yet.
- Facebook: Active but not converting Google traffic. Frame: Facebook is great for awareness - Google is where people go when they are ready to spend money.
- Google Ads: Are they converting it? Frame: How is the ROI? Most people find ads are expensive unless the landing page is right.
- My website: Probe deeper. Nice! How many enquiries would you say you get a month from it?
- Nothing or I am not sure: Clear opening. That is really common, honestly. Most businesses are invisible online without meaning to be.

### Question 3: What would change for your business if you had a steady flow of enquiries online?
This is the future-pacing question. Gets them to imagine a better reality and attach emotion to your solution BEFORE you have presented it.

Common answers: I could stop relying on word of mouth / I could be more selective about jobs / I could actually plan my schedule / I could hire someone / I would stop stressing about slow periods.

Lock it in: That is exactly what we help with. Can I show you how it works?

## Opening Conversation Scorecard

After every opening, score yourself:
- Did I ask more than I talked? /10
- Did I use their exact words back at them? /10
- Did I find out how they currently get clients? /10
- Did I earn the right to present a solution? /10

Aim for 7+ across all four. If you are talking more than they are, you have lost the opening.

---

## Common Opening Mistakes

Mistake 1: Leading with We do websites and SEO
Fix: Lead with a question about their business instead.

Mistake 2: Explaining your product before understanding their problem
Fix: Ask all 3 questions before mentioning any product features.

Mistake 3: Being too formal or corporate
Fix: Match their energy. Brisbane business owners are casual. Be human.

Mistake 4: Accepting not interested at the opener
Fix: No worries at all! Can I ask one quick question before I let you go - are you getting much from Google at the moment?

Mistake 5: Asking what is your budget
Fix: Never. See Module 03 for how to navigate budget conversations correctly.

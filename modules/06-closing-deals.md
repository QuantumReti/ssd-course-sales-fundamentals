# Module 06: Closing Deals

## The Psychology of Closing

Most salespeople are afraid of the close. But here is the truth: if you have done the qualification and presentation well, closing is just the natural next step. The close is not a trick. It is giving someone permission to say yes to something they already want.

Signs they are ready to close:
- How does it work? / What do I need to do?
- How quickly can you do this?
- Do you have examples I can look at?
- So the monthly cost is 119?
- Any question about implementation or logistics

When you hear these, stop presenting and start closing.

---

## Close 1: The Assumptive Close

Treats the sale as already decided and moves to the next logical step. It does not create a decision moment - it just moves things forward.

Script:
So the next step is getting your domain sorted. Are you happy with businessname.com.au or would you like to look at a few options?

Other assumptive close examples:
- Let me grab your details to get this started - what is the best email to send the invoice to?
- I will get you set up now. Is the business name [name] or do you trade under something different?
- Perfect - I will send you the payment link now and we will be in touch within a couple of hours to confirm the brief.

The key: assume and move forward. Do not ask do you want to go ahead? - ask the question that comes after yes.

---

## Close 2: The Urgency Close

Create a legitimate reason to act now rather than later. Always be honest - manufactured urgency damages trust.

Legitimate urgency scenarios:
- We have 3 onboarding slots this week
- We are at capacity until [date] - if you want to be live before [event/season], now is the time
- We have just had a price review - I can lock in the 199 for you but I cannot guarantee it past this week

Script:
I want to be upfront with you - we have got 3 spots open this week for new builds and we are filling up fast. If you want to be live in the next few days, it makes sense to lock it in today. I would hate for you to wait another 2-3 weeks if you are ready to go.

Use sparingly. If you cry urgency every conversation, it loses meaning.

---

## Close 3: The Summary Close

Recap everything agreed upon, then ask for the close. This reminds them of all the value they have already mentally committed to.

Script:
So let me just recap what we have talked about. You are a [trade/business type] in [suburb], you want to be found on Google, and you need it done fast without paying agency prices.

Here is what we are doing for you: professional website built and live in 24 hours (199 one-off), fast hosting and security (20 per month), and ongoing local SEO to push you up Google (99 per month). Total investment: 119 a month after today.

Based on everything we have discussed, does that sound like the right move for your business?

---

## Close 4: The Calendar Close

For prospects who are not quite ready to pay today but are clearly interested.

Script:
It sounds like you are keen - you just want to have a proper chat and get your questions answered. That makes total sense.

Let me send you a link to grab 10 minutes with Josh - he is our founder and handles the builds personally for new clients. He will walk you through the whole thing, answer anything I have not covered, and you can decide from there. No obligation. Does that work?

Use this when:
- The deal is too complex for Mason to close alone (AI Jetpack, full package)
- The prospect wants to speak to a human
- They have technical questions beyond Mason's scope
- The deal is large enough to warrant Josh's personal attention

After sending the calendar link, follow up within 4 hours if they have not booked:
Hey! Did you get the link okay? Just want to make sure it came through.

## What to Do When They Say Yes

### Immediately:
1. Confirm the key details: Great! Can I grab your full name, email address, and best phone number?
2. Send the payment link immediately - do not let time pass
3. Set clear expectations: You will get a confirmation email and we will reach out within [X hours] to gather your brief. It is quick - usually 10-15 minutes.
4. Give them a next step: The main thing you will need is your logo if you have one, and a few photos of your work. If you do not have those, no worries - we can work with what is available.

### Within 1 hour:
- Send a WhatsApp confirmation: Awesome - invoice is on its way! Excited to get you set up. You will hear from us [timeframe].
- Log the sale in the CRM
- Tag them as New Client - Onboarding

### What NOT to do:
- Do not ask unnecessary questions that slow the momentum
- Do not re-pitch anything - they have already decided
- Do not make them feel like the process is complicated
- Do not let post-yes silence go on - fill it with warmth and next steps

## The 48-Hour Rule

If a prospect does not close within 48 hours of a serious conversation, the probability of closing drops significantly.

The rule:
- Close in the conversation, or set a specific next step within 48 hours
- If they are thinking about it, get a specific follow-up time: When should I check back in - tomorrow morning or Wednesday?
- If 48 hours pass with no response to a follow-up, send one more gentle message and move on

The 48-hour follow-up script:
Hey [Name] - just circling back from our chat [X days ago]. I know you were thinking it over - any questions I can help with? Happy to jump on a quick call if easier.

## Closing Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Asking do you want to go ahead? or are you ready to buy?
These create a moment of resistance. Use assumptive language instead.

Mistake 2: Talking after asking the closing question
Ask the question, then stop. The first person to talk after a closing question loses. Wait it out.

Mistake 3: Discounting to close
If they ask for a discount: I can check what we can do, but I want to make sure the price is not the real concern - is there something else that does not feel right?

Mistake 4: Closing too early
If they have not felt the problem deeply and seen the solution clearly, the close will feel pushy. Do the work in Modules 03 and 04 first.

Mistake 5: Giving up after one no
Most first objections are soft. Handle it once, then try again: I understand. Setting that aside for a moment - is there anything else holding you back?

|----------|-------------------|

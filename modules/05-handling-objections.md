# Module 05: Handling Objections

## Objections Are Not Rejections

Every objection is a request for more information or reassurance. When someone raises an objection, they are still engaged. Your job is to hear it, validate it, and reframe it without dismissing their concern.

The 3-Step Objection Handle:
1. Acknowledge - Show you heard them and it is a valid concern
2. Reframe - Shift the perspective without invalidating their position
3. Advance - Move the conversation forward with a question or next step

---

## Objection 1: It Is Too Expensive

What they are really saying: I am not sure the value justifies the cost.

The Handle:
I hear you. Can I ask - expensive compared to what?

Here is how I look at it: one new job from Google - one call that turns into a booking - that is easily 300, 500, 1,000+ depending on your trade. Our website setup is 199. That is less than one job.

So the question is not really whether 199 is expensive. The question is: do you think Google will send you at least one job in the first year? If yes - it pays for itself in the first month.

If they are objecting to the monthly cost:
The 119 a month - let us break that down. That is 4 a day. If we bring you one extra job a month from Google - and most of our clients get far more than that - this is the cheapest marketing you will ever do.

Key principle: Never defend the price directly. Make them do the math themselves.

---

## Objection 2: I Already Have a Website

What they are really saying: I do not see why I need a new one.

The Handle:
That is great - can I ask, is it actually bringing you clients right now, or more just sitting there as a digital business card?

[Wait for answer]

Yeah, that is really common. Most websites - especially ones built more than 2-3 years ago - are not set up to rank on Google. They look fine, but Google cannot really see them properly. A website that is not bringing in enquiries is costing you money, not making you money.

Can I have a quick look at yours? I can tell you in about 30 seconds whether it is working for you or just existing.

If they say it is already bringing in clients:
That is awesome - good sign that your market is online. The question then is whether it could be doing even more. What does your Google review count look like? And do you know where you rank for [service] in [suburb]?

---

## Objection 3: I Need to Think About It

What they are really saying (one of):
- I am not 100% sure yet - need more information
- I want to compare options - need differentiation
- I do not feel enough urgency - need a reason to act now
- I am not the decision-maker - need to check with someone

The Handle:
Totally fair - I would never want you to feel rushed. Can I ask what specifically you need to feel confident moving forward? Is it more information, or is it more about timing?

If it is timing: Makes sense. Just so I know when to follow up - are you thinking a week, a month, or it really depends?

If it is just wanting to think:
Of course. While you are thinking - is there anything about the offer itself that does not feel right? Sometimes when people say they want to think, there is one specific thing holding them back and I would rather just address it directly.

This last question surfaces hidden objections. Most of the time, thinking about it is a polite cover for an unaddressed concern.

---

## Objection 4: I Will Ask My Business Partner or Spouse

The Handle:
Of course - that makes complete sense. When you chat tonight, what do you think their biggest concern will be? Is it more the cost side of things, or making sure it is going to actually work?

This does two things:
1. Helps you understand the real objection - usually what they say the partner will ask IS what they are thinking
2. Arms them to become your internal champion

Would it help if I put together a quick one-pager you can show them? Something that covers what they get, the cost, and the results we have seen for similar businesses?

If the partner is genuinely involved:
Is there a good time to jump on a quick call with both of you? Even 10 minutes - I can answer any questions directly and make it easier.

---

## Objection 5: I Tried Ads or a Website Before and It Did Not Work

What they are really saying: I have been burned before and I am scared it will happen again.

This objection deserves respect. Do not dismiss it.

The Handle:
I completely understand - that is honestly really common, and it is frustrating. Can I ask what happened? Was it the website itself, or the agency you worked with?

[Listen fully]

Yeah, that is the classic agency story. They take the money, do the build, and then you never hear from them again. The site looks good on day one and then just sits there.

What we do differently is [specific differentiator: 24-hour builds, direct support, ongoing SEO built in, Google review system etc.]. And honestly - at 199 to get started, your risk is less than taking a client to lunch. If it does not work, you have lost 199. If it does work, you have unlocked a new revenue stream.

Key move: Acknowledge the bad experience fully before presenting your solution. If you pivot too fast, they feel dismissed.

---

## Objection 6: I Do Not Need a Website - I Get All My Work From Referrals

What they are really saying: Things are going OK so I do not feel the urgency.

The Handle:
That is great - referrals are the best leads you can get. But can I ask you something? When someone refers you to their mate, what do you think happens next?

[Pause]

Almost always, they Google you before they call. They want to check you out - see your reviews, see your work, make sure you are legit. If they land on a dated website, a blank Google listing, or nothing at all... they might still call. Or they might call the next person on the list who looks more established.

You are not losing all your referrals. But you might be losing 10-20% of them - and you would never know. That is silent churn.

And the other question is: what happens to your business if your main referral source slows down? Referrals are fantastic but they are not predictable. Having Google working for you is like having a second referral network that runs 24/7.

## The Objection Handling Framework

| Objection | Acknowledge | Reframe | Advance |
|-----------|-------------|---------|---------|
| Too expensive | I hear you | ROI: one job covers a year | Do you think Google will send you one job? |
| Already have a website | That is great | Is it working? | Can I take a quick look? |
| Need to think | Totally fair | Find the specific concern | What specifically would make you confident? |
| Need to ask partner | Of course | Arm them as champion | What do you think their main question will be? |
| Bad experience | I understand | Acknowledge fully, differentiate | What happened? |
| Referral dependent | Referrals are gold | Silent churn risk | What do you think happens when they Google you? |

---

## What NOT to Do When Handling Objections

1. Do not argue - you will not win a debate with a prospect
2. Do not immediately counter - pause and acknowledge first
3. Do not over-explain - one strong reframe beats three weak ones
4. Do not cave to fake pressure - never discount without reason
5. Do not get defensive - if they have had a bad experience, acknowledge the industry's failures

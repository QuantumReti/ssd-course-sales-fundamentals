# Module 04: Presenting Solutions

## The Cardinal Rule: Outcomes First, Features Never

Business owners do not buy websites. They do not buy SEO. They buy more phone calls, more bookings, more jobs on the calendar.

Wrong approach: We build responsive, mobile-optimised websites with on-page SEO, Google indexing, and a custom domain.

Right approach: After we launch, when someone in [suburb] Googles [their service], your name comes up. They see your reviews, your photos, your phone number - and they call you instead of your competitor.

Every feature must be translated into an outcome first. Features are proof. Outcomes are the reason they buy.

## The Before and After Frame

The most powerful presentation structure creates a vivid contrast between their current painful reality and the better world your solution delivers.

Structure:
1. Describe the before - their current problem in their words
2. Describe the after - the outcome they will experience with your solution
3. Bridge the gap - here is how we get you from there to here

### Example for a Plumber
Right now, when someone in [suburb] has a burst pipe and Googles emergency plumber [suburb], they are probably finding [competitor] or one of the big guys. You are not showing up - or you are on page 2, which is basically invisible.

After we launch your site - properly set up, with your Google Business Profile optimised - that same search shows your name, your reviews, your contact number. They call you. That is the difference we are talking about.

We get this done in 24 hours, for 199. You own the site. No contract.

Tip: Before your presentation, Google [their service] [their suburb] and screenshot what comes up. Then show them: This is what someone sees right now when they search for your type of business here. You are not in this picture.

## Price Anchoring

Brisbane SMBs have been quoted by agencies. They have seen 5,000, 10,000, 15,000+ proposals. That is their reference point - and it makes our price look absurdly good.

But only if we set the anchor first.

Wrong order: 199 ... Most agencies charge 5K+ (feels like a discount pitch)
Right order: Most agencies charge 5,000-15,000 for a professional website ... we do it for 199, live in 24 hours. (feels like a revelation)

### The Anchor Script
Have you had any quotes done before, or spoken to any agencies?

If yes: What were they coming in at? ... Yeah, 5-10K is pretty standard for a full agency build. We have completely disrupted that model. We get you the same quality result - professional, mobile-optimised, SEO-ready - for 199. Live in 24 hours.

If no: The typical agency quote in Brisbane for a professional website is somewhere between 5,000 and 15,000. For a small business that is a huge investment with a long lead time. We have built a system that does this for 199. Live in 24 hours.

The anchor makes 199 feel like a cheat code. Do not skip it.

## The Value Stack Presentation

Never present the price in isolation. Stack the value first, then reveal the price.

Here is what you are getting:
- Professional website - written, designed, and built for you (199 one-time)
- Hosting and security - your site is live, fast, and protected (20 per month)
- Local SEO - we optimise your site and Google listing so people can actually find you (99 per month)

So for 119 a month after setup, you have got a complete online presence that is actively working to bring you clients. That is less than a weekly grocery shop - and it is bringing you jobs worth hundreds or thousands of dollars each.

The comparison frame - always anchor the monthly cost against something familiar and small:
- Less than a weekly grocery shop
- Less than a tank of fuel
- Less than one no-show on your calendar costs you
- Less than one job you would get from it pays for 3 months

## Using Social Proof

The Proof Formula:
We just did this for a [similar business type] in [Brisbane suburb]. They were in the same situation - [mirror their pain point]. Within [timeframe], [specific result].

Specific Proof Examples:

Tradies: We worked with a plumber in the northern suburbs - he had 6 Google reviews and his competitor had 140. Three months after launching with us, he had 47 reviews, was ranking number 3 in his suburb, and he told me his phone had doubled.

Health/Allied Health: A physio clinic in Brisbane came to us with a website from 2015 that was not mobile-friendly. We rebuilt it, sorted their Google profile, and they went from 8 new patient enquiries a month to over 30.

Cafes/Hospitality: A cafe we worked with in the inner south started posting on Google Business twice a week after we set it up. Their foot traffic on weekday mornings - their slowest time - went up noticeably just from people finding them on Maps.

Key rules for social proof:
1. Be specific - industry, location, numbers, timeframe
2. Mirror their situation - the more similar, the more powerful
3. Do not exaggerate - one credible story beats three implausible ones
4. If possible, offer a reference with the client's permission

## The Presentation Sequence

Follow this order every time:
1. Recap their situation - So from what you have told me, [restate their problem in their words]. Is that right?
2. Paint the after - Here is what life looks like after we sort this
3. Anchor the price - Most agencies charge 5K-15K for this
4. Stack the value - Here is everything you are getting
5. Reveal your price - For 199 to get started, and 119 per month ongoing
6. Deploy social proof - We just did this for a [similar business]
7. Ask for the close - Does that make sense for what you are trying to achieve?

## Presentation Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Listing features without outcomes
Fix: Every feature must be followed by which means...
Mobile-optimised - which means it looks perfect on every phone, and Google ranks it higher.

Mistake 2: Presenting everything at once
Fix: Start with the core (website). Add on only if they are interested.

Mistake 3: Skipping the anchor
Fix: Always reference the agency price before revealing yours.

Mistake 4: Rushing through the value stack
Fix: Slow down. Let each item land. Pause after the total.

Mistake 5: Apologising for the price
Fix: Present with confidence. For 199 - not it is only 199 or just 199. Hedging signals insecurity.

# Module 03: Qualifying Leads

## Why Qualification Matters

Not every prospect is worth pursuing. Some will waste your time and never close. Others are ready to buy today. The faster you tell the difference, the more money you make.

---

## The BANT Framework (Adapted for SMBs)

BANT stands for Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline.

---

### B - Budget

The rule: Never ask what is your budget?

Asking directly puts people on the defensive. Qualify budget indirectly instead.

Indirect budget questions:
- Are you looking to invest in your online presence this month, or more exploring your options right now?
- Are you at the point where you are ready to move forward, or still doing your research?
- Is budget a concern for you, or is it more about making sure you pick the right option?

Reading the signals:
- We are ready to move = budget is there, needs confidence
- Just exploring = budget may not be confirmed, needs more nurturing
- It depends on the price = budget uncertain, lean on value stack before quoting

Our price is accessible but it still needs framing. At 199 for a website, most business owners CAN afford it. Your job is to make sure they are mentally ready to invest.

---

### A - Authority

You must be talking to the decision-maker.

Is it just yourself making the call on this, or is there a business partner involved?

If there is another decision-maker: Makes sense. When you chat with them, what do you think their biggest question will be? I want to make sure you have got everything you need to answer it.

This identifies the second decision-maker AND turns your contact into your internal champion.

Red flag: Prospect keeps saying I need to check with someone but will not tell you who or when. Often means they are not the real buyer or they are stalling.

---

### N - Need

Need scoring: Rate urgency 1-10 based on their answers.

High-need signals (Score 7-10):
- My competitor is dominating Google and I am losing work
- We just opened and need to get our name out there fast
- Our old website is broken and I have been getting calls about it
- I am paying for Google Ads but have no website to send people to
- I had a Google listing but it got hacked or taken down

Medium-need signals (Score 4-6):
- I know I should probably have a better website
- Someone mentioned I should look into this
- My website is a bit outdated

Low-need signals (Score 1-3):
- I am doing fine with referrals
- I am not really focused on this right now
- I am just having a look

Increasing need: You can raise urgency by helping them quantify the problem.

If someone Googles [their service] in [their suburb] right now, do you know where you come up? Would you like me to check that while we are talking?

Actually showing them they are invisible turns a medium-need into a high-need instantly.

---

### T - Timeline

Is this something you are looking to get done in the next week or two, or is it more of a longer-term project?

Reading timeline signals:
- ASAP or this week = Hot lead, move fast
- Next month = Warm, keep engaged, create a reason to act sooner
- Eventually = Nurture - do not over-invest time now
- Not sure = Usually not a priority - probe the need harder before investing

Creating urgency ethically: The reason I ask is we have got a few spots open this week and I would hate for you to wait an extra 2-3 weeks. If you are happy with what we have talked about, it makes sense to lock it in while we can move fast.

Only use this if it is true. Manufactured urgency damages trust.

---

## Red Flags: Time-Waster Signals

### I am just looking or just exploring
Keep it shorter, lighter, and move to value faster.

### My nephew or cousin or mate does websites
That is great - do they do ongoing SEO and Google ranking as well, or just the build? Because that is usually where the real value is.

### We had a bad experience with an agency before
Not a disqualifier - address it head-on. Can I ask what happened? I want to make sure we do not repeat that.

### Can you just send me some information?
Of course! To make sure I send the right thing - what is the biggest thing you are trying to solve for your business right now?

### I want to think about it (in qualifying phase)
Go back and deepen the need. Do not present until they feel the pain.

---

## Green Flags: Hot Lead Signals

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| My competitor is getting all the calls | Active competitive pain - high urgency |
| We just opened | Needs online presence immediately |
| Our old site is broken | Functional problem, ready to solve |
| I have been meaning to sort this for months | Psychologically ready, just needs a nudge |
| How quickly can you do this? | Buying signal - they are picturing ownership |
| What do I need to do? | Close-ready - move to next steps immediately |
| Do you have any Brisbane businesses I can look at? | Social proof seek - they are almost there |
| That sounds pretty reasonable | Price acceptance - assume the close |

## How to Disqualify Without Burning the Relationship

### Timing Not Right
Totally get it - timing is everything. I will check back in with you in X weeks. In the meantime, if anything changes or the competitor situation gets worse, reach out anytime.

### Budget Not Available
No stress at all. When budget frees up, we will still be here - and the price will not change. I will send you a quick summary so you have got it handy.

The rule: Never make someone feel dumb or small for not being ready. Brisbane is a small business community. The person you do not close today sends you 3 referrals next year if you treated them right.

---

## The Qualification Summary Card

After every qualifying conversation, answer these 5 questions:
1. Budget: Are they mentally ready to invest?
2. Authority: Am I talking to the decision-maker?
3. Need: Score 1-10 - how urgent is their problem?
4. Timeline: When are they looking to act?
5. Fit: Are they the type of business we serve well?

Close-ready: Budget yes + Authority yes + Need 7+ + Timeline within 2 weeks = Move to solution presentation now.
Nurture: Any two of the above missing = Schedule follow-up, provide value, re-qualify in 2 weeks.
Disqualify: No need, no authority, or clearly not a fit = Exit warmly, log, move on.

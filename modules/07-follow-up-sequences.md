# Module 07: Follow-Up Sequences

## Why Most Sales Are Lost in the Follow-Up

80% of sales require 5 or more follow-ups, yet the majority of salespeople give up after 1 or 2 attempts. This is where most of the money is left on the table.

Brisbane business owners are busy. A message they did not respond to yesterday might be exactly what they needed to see today. Consistent, value-driven follow-up is not annoying - it is professional persistence.

Each touch should:
- Be short and human (not a marketing email)
- Deliver value OR create a reason to re-engage
- Have one clear call to action
- Never feel desperate or pressured

---

## The Full Follow-Up Sequence

### Day 0 (Same day as conversation)
Goal: Capture while warm, provide easy path to purchase.

Hey [Name]! Great chatting with you today. Here is a quick summary of what we talked about:
- Professional website - 199 (one-off)
- Hosting and security - 20 per month
- Local SEO - 99 per month
- Total ongoing: 119 per month

When you are ready to get started, here is the link: [payment link]

Happy to answer any questions - just reply here. Chat soon!

Why it works: summarises the value, makes the next step frictionless, does not pressure.

---

### Day 1 (24 hours later)
Goal: Keep the conversation alive, invite questions.

Hey [Name]! Just checking in - any questions from what we talked about yesterday? Happy to clarify anything or grab 5 mins on a call if easier.

Short. Human. No pressure. Opens the door without pushing.

---

### Day 3
Goal: Social proof touch - make it relevant to them.

Hey [Name] - thought of you when I saw this. We just launched a site for a [similar business type] in Brisbane - they were in pretty much the same spot as you. [One specific result - e.g., First week live and they had 3 enquiries from Google].

Happy to share it if you want to see what yours could look like.

Alternative Day 3 touch - relevant insight:
Quick thought for [their business type] - Google is prioritising businesses with fresh photos on their Google listing. Most tradies or cafes have not updated theirs in years. Worth 5 mins to add a couple of recent shots. Anyway - still here if you want to get yours sorted properly.

---

### Day 7
Goal: Deliver a piece of genuine value specific to their industry.

Hey [Name] - one quick tip for [their trade or industry] on Google. The businesses ranking number 1 in most Brisbane suburbs all have one thing in common: they reply to every review - even the bad ones. Google sees this as an activity signal and it helps rankings.

If you want to sort your online presence properly, I am still here. Just say the word.

This touch should feel like a friend sharing useful info, not a sales follow-up.

Industry-specific tips to rotate:

For plumbers or electricians or tradies:
Quick Google tip for [trade]: emergency [trade] Brisbane gets thousands of searches a month. If your site mentions your emergency service and your Google listing has emergency in the description, you capture those calls. Easy win.

For dentists or physios or health:
For health businesses on Google - booking links directly on your Google Business Profile can increase appointment bookings by 20-30%. Most clinics do not have this set up. Super quick fix if you want me to show you how.

For cafes or hospitality:
Did you know Google Business now lets you add your menu directly to your Maps listing? People looking for lunch click on yours, see the menu, and decide to come in - without ever visiting your website.

---

### Day 14
Goal: Soft final check-in before moving to nurture.

Hey [Name] - just wanted to do one last check-in. I know timing is not always right and that is completely fine.

If this is something you want to revisit down the track, just give me a shout - I will still be here. No pressure at all.

Either way, hope the business is going well!

This message signals you are not going to hound them. This actually makes people MORE likely to respond, because the pressure they have been avoiding is now gone.

Often gets replies like:
- Sorry I have been flat out - let us do it (immediate close)
- I will come back to you in a month (set a reminder)
- Thanks, not right now (move to monthly nurture)

## When to Let Go

After 5 follow-up touches with no engagement, stop active follow-up.

The exit message:
Hey [Name] - I will not keep following up after this, do not worry. If it ever makes sense down the track, you know where I am. Good luck with the business!

The casualness usually gets a reply - and sometimes even a close. People respond when they feel the pressure lift.

After this: tag the lead as Nurture and move to monthly email only.

---

## Monthly Nurture Email

For leads who have not converted after the active sequence, add them to a monthly value email.

Format:
- Subject: Something interesting and relevant (The 199 trick Brisbane plumbers are using to win online)
- Content: 1 local business tip + 1 client success story + soft call to action
- Length: Under 200 words
- Tone: Conversational, not corporate

These emails do their work quietly over months. The prospect who ignored you in March often replies in August when their competitor launches a new website and they finally feel the urgency.

## Re-Engagement Triggers

Even in nurture mode, there are specific moments to reach out proactively:

Trigger 1: New Service Launch
Hey [Name] - we just launched something new that I think is perfect for [their industry]. We are now offering [new service] - which basically means [plain English benefit]. Want to hear more?

Trigger 2: Seasonal Trigger
Hey! Spring is around the corner - if you are in landscaping or HVAC or building, this is prime time to get your Google presence sorted before the rush. We can get you live in 24 hours.

Trigger 3: Competitor Alert
Hey [Name] - noticed [competitor] just launched a new website and is pushing hard on Google in [suburb]. Thought you would want to know. If you want to get ahead of them, now is a good time.

Trigger 4: News or Event Tie-In
Hey! Saw that [relevant local event or regulation or trend]. For [their industry], this usually means more enquiry coming. Good time to make sure you are showing up when people search.

## Follow-Up Tone Guide

| Day | Tone | Length | Goal |
|-----|------|--------|------|
| Day 0 | Warm and clear | Medium | Get them to act now |
| Day 1 | Casual check-in | Short | Keep door open |
| Day 3 | Value plus proof | Medium | Build confidence |
| Day 7 | Genuine tip | Short | Position as trusted advisor |
| Day 14 | Soft and human | Short | Final active touch |
| Monthly | Informative | Short | Stay top of mind |

## Follow-Up Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Following up with just checking in repeatedly
This adds zero value. Every touch should give them something useful.

Mistake 2: Following up too frequently in the first 48 hours
Two messages in 12 hours feels desperate. Respect the timeline.

Mistake 3: Copy-paste generic follow-ups
Personalise every message. Use their name, their industry, their specific situation.

Mistake 4: Giving up at touch 2
Most salespeople do this. The conversion window is 5-7 touches for most SMB sales. Persistence with value wins.

Mistake 5: Making every follow-up about closing
Follow-ups should build the relationship. The close is a byproduct of trust, not the subject of every message.
